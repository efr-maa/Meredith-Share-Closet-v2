About Meredith Share Closet

Meredith Share Closet is a campus sharing project created to help students donate useful items and find items they may need. The goal is to make sharing easier, reduce waste, and support students in a kind and respectful way.

Why I Created This Project

As an international student, I've learned firsthand how important it is to manage money carefully. International transfer fees, exchange rates, and other financial hurdles mean my parents aren't always able to send much for everyday living expenses. That experience taught me that I don't always need to buy everything new.

Many international students face similar situations — but this need isn't unique to us. Domestic students also deal with the cost of housing, school supplies, clothing, and everyday college life. Meredith Share Closet exists to support any student who could benefit from shared resources, regardless of background.

This idea was also inspired by generous campus efforts such as Daisy Trade and supportive departments on campus, including areas such as the Martin and Business School spaces that help students with low-budget needs. Meredith Share Closet continues that same spirit of care, sharing, and community support.

Why This Project Matters

College students often have items they no longer use, such as clothes, books, dorm supplies, or shoes. Instead of throwing those items away, Meredith Share Closet gives students a simple way to pass them on to someone else.

This project helps students save money, reduce waste, and feel less alone when they need support. A jacket, a pair of shoes, a book, or a dorm item may seem small, but it can make a meaningful difference for someone trying to manage college life.

How the Closet Works

Browse
Students view available items by category.
Request
Students complete a request form with their contact and pickup information.
Donate
Students submit details about items they want to donate.
Pick Up
Students arrange a campus pickup time after their request is reviewed.

Student Reflections

A few thoughts shared by students when asked what a campus sharing closet could mean to them:

“This kind of project would help students feel seen and supported, especially when money is tight.”

“Sometimes students need small things, but buying everything new can be hard. A sharing closet makes campus feel more caring.”

“I would feel grateful knowing another student cared enough to donate something I needed.”
